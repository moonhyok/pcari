Many existing Philippine government initiated practices take a top down approach to knowledge sharing, treating local communities as passive clients instead of active partners. Bottom up e-government approaches that actively engage local community members, combined with emerging ICT technologies, have great potential to enhance existing practices and effectively identify and address the perspectives, motivations and priorities of local communities. Providing an electronic and participatory assessment digital toolkit could improve policymaking processes as well as make government policies more relevant to citizens.
